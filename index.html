Subject-verb agreement


Convention/Grammar

Subject-verb agreement is a fundamental rule in English grammar that enhances clarity and correctness in communication.

Singular subjects pair with singular verbs, while plural subjects require plural verbs.

When using "either...or" or "neither...nor," the verb agrees with the subject nearest to it.

Indefinite pronouns can be tricky. Most take a singular verb, but both, few, many, others and several take a plural verb.

A compound subject is two or more nouns, pronouns, or noun phrases acting together as the subject of a clause or sentence.

Sentence: The land hereabouts is hard on its farmers.
